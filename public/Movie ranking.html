The Brutalist

The Brutalist (2024), directed by Brady Corbet, is an epic period drama that follows Hungarian-Jewish architect László Tóth (Adrien Brody) and his wife Erzsébet (Felicity Jones) as they escape post-war Europe to rebuild their lives in America. Set against the rise of brutalist architecture, the film explores themes of ambition, identity, and displacement, as László's talents draw the attention of a powerful industrialist. Visually striking and emotionally layered, it has received critical acclaim for its direction, performances, and powerful storytelling.


Sing Sing

Sing Sing (2023) is a powerful prison drama directed by Greg Kwedar, starring Colman Domingo as Divine G, a man wrongfully incarcerated who finds hope and redemption through a theater program at Sing Sing Correctional Facility. Based on true events and featuring real formerly incarcerated individuals in its cast, the film explores themes of identity, healing, and the transformative power of art. Praised for its authenticity and emotional depth, Sing Sing premiered at the Toronto International Film Festival and was released by A24 in July 2024, earning critical acclaim and multiple award nominations.


I'm still here

I'm Still Here (2024) is a Brazilian drama directed by Walter Salles, based on the true story of Eunice Paiva, whose husband, former congressman Rubens Paiva, was forcibly disappeared during Brazil’s military dictatorship in the 1970s. The film stars Fernanda Torres and Selton Mello, with Fernanda Montenegro as the older Eunice. Praised for its emotional depth and powerful performances, it premiered at the Venice Film Festival and became the highest-grossing Brazilian film post-pandemic. The film earned multiple award nominations, including Best Picture and Best International Feature.
